The night security job paid double. Now I know why.
subreddit: nosleep

The listing said overnight security at an empty office building. Twelve bucks an hour sounded low until I read the bonus: double pay, paid in cash, for following three rules exactly.

Rule one: stay in the lobby. Rule two: every camera feed must show an empty hallway. Rule three: if a feed shows a person, do not go look. Just write down the time and keep watching.

The first three nights were nothing. Boring, quiet, the hum of the vending machine. On the fourth night, at 3:14 a.m., camera six showed a man standing at the end of the second-floor hall. Facing the wall. Not moving.

I remembered the rule. I wrote down the time. My hands were shaking but I kept watching. At 3:15 he was closer to the camera. At 3:16 he was closer still. I never saw him take a step. He was just nearer in each glance, the way the minute hand moves when you look away.

By 3:19 his face filled the entire screen, pressed against the lens, eyes wide open and dry. Then camera six went black.

The phone on the desk rang. I picked it up. A calm voice said, "You followed the rules. Don't ever break them." Then a dial tone.

I have one more shift this week. The cash is already in an envelope with my name on it. I keep telling myself I'll quit. I keep showing up.
