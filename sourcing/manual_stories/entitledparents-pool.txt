Entitled mom demanded I leave the pool I rented for my daughter's birthday
subreddit: entitledparents

For my daughter's birthday I rented out our community pool for a private two-hour party. Paid the fee, got the permit, posted the "private event" sign the office gave me. Twelve kids, balloons, the whole thing.

Forty minutes in, a woman strolls in with three kids and starts setting up chairs. I politely explain it's a private party and show her the permit. She says, "Pools are public. You can't reserve nature." I point out it's a man-made community pool, not nature, and that she needs to leave.

She refuses and tells her kids to "jump in and ignore the mean man." That's when the lifeguard, who I'd given a copy of the permit to, walks over. The woman tries the same speech on him.

He calmly radios the front office. The office calls building security. Security informs her that trespassing on a permitted private event can get her membership suspended. Her face went white. She packed up so fast she left a cooler behind. We used her abandoned cooler to keep the juice boxes cold for the rest of the party.
