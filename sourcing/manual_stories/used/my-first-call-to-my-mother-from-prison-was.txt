My first call to my mother from prison was hilarious.
subreddit: confession
We barely got through the hellos. I had to tell her something I thought she'd find interesting. A lady she knew from our hometown was my across the aisle bottom bunkie. She had murdered her husband. I was in there on an F5. My mother's words, "YOU'RE IN THERE WITH MURDERES!"

"MOM! They didn't sentence me to summer camp."

Next phone call was her telling me the salon was robbed by a guy with what, "she said was a fake gun." She refused to open the register. So he stole the whole damn register. I was angry at the time and told her people would kill you for $20! Argued back and forth about it. My brother was mad because he had to buy a new register.

I was lucky enough to have 8 1/2 more years with her after I got out. Lots of trauma beyond our control created a dark sense of humor in our family. We joked about the day before she died in 2017.

Edited to add for those that read my comment about the meth bust close by when she asked the cops not to park in the salons parking lot.....

My SO now was actually the guy that supplied the sudafed pills the guy was cooking meth with. I am happy to say he is almost 6 years clean come August! This is the small-town life!
