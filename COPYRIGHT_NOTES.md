# Copyright & Monetization Notes (READ BEFORE GOING PUBLIC)

The goal: a channel that can actually be monetized. That means every
asset must be either (a) something you own, or (b) licensed for
commercial reuse. Don't gamble with this — one Content ID claim can
demonetize a video; a pattern of it can sink the channel.

## 1. Background footage — SAFE sources only

NEVER use: ripped Subway Surfers / Minecraft parkour / GTA clips, or
anything downloaded from another creator's YouTube/TikTok. Even if a
channel says "no copyright," that is not a real license — the game
publisher or original uploader can still claim it.

SAFE options (commercial use, no/low attribution):
- **Pexels Videos**   https://www.pexels.com/videos/   (free, commercial OK, no attribution)
- **Pixabay Videos**  https://pixabay.com/videos/       (free, commercial OK, no attribution)
- **Mixkit**          https://mixkit.co/free-stock-video/ (free, commercial OK, check item license)
- **Coverr**          https://coverr.co/                 (free, commercial OK)
- **NASA**            https://images.nasa.gov/           (public domain space footage)
- **Footage you record yourself** (your phone, your own gameplay) — you own it outright.

Good background types for this niche: calming nature, satisfying
loops (soap cutting, hydraulic press), abstract motion, slow drone
shots, or gameplay you legally recorded yourself. Vertical or
landscape both fine — we crop to 9:16.

NOTE on "gameplay": recording your OWN gameplay is fine for most games
whose terms allow content creation (Minecraft, most indies). Do NOT
download someone else's gameplay video.

## 2. YouTube "reused / repetitive content" policy

YouTube can refuse monetization for low-effort repackaged content.
AgentDrop adds transformative value via:
- original AI narration (not the original audio)
- synced on-screen captions
- our own vertical format + editing
Strengthen further by:
- rotating among several different background clips
- adding a short spoken hook at the start
- varying caption style / using different voices occasionally
- not posting near-identical videos back to back

## 3. Reddit content

Stories are owned by their authors (plus Reddit's content license).
Best practices to reduce risk and stay ethical:
- credit the source subreddit in the description (AgentDrop does this)
- treat it as transformative commentary, not a straight copy
- consider light paraphrasing of distinctive passages
- honor takedown requests promptly

## 4. Music (if you add any later)

Only use royalty-free / commercially-licensed music (YouTube Audio
Library, Pixabay Music). Never use popular songs.
